Trashbags Co. — System Overview

Automated Weekly Ad Intelligence

Every Sunday morning, this system wakes up, pulls data from 5 sources, runs it through an AI, and emails you a plain-English report telling you exactly what to do with your ads that week. No dashboards to check, no spreadsheets, no agency.

✅ Fully automated · 📧 Delivered to your inbox every Sunday · 🔒 Read-only — never touches your ad spend · 🧠 Powered by Claude AI
What It Does — In Plain English
The problem it solves: Managing Meta ads for a seasonal brand means tracking snow conditions, competitor moves, event calendars, and search trends — all at once, across dozens of markets. Doing that manually every week takes hours and most of it gets missed.
What this system does instead: Every Sunday it automatically checks your ad account, looks at what your competitors are running, checks snow conditions at 12 resorts, scans Google search trends across 36 target cities, and looks up upcoming park events. It feeds all of that into an AI (Claude) which analyzes it and writes you a short report telling you which ads to pause, which to turn up, where to launch new ones, and what's happening in the market this week.
What you do with it: Read the Game Plan section (takes about 3 minutes), make the moves in your Meta Ads account, done. The system never touches your budget or makes any changes itself — it only reads and reports.
How It Works — Step by Step
1
Every Sunday at 8am — data collection starts
The system automatically pulls from all 5 data sources at the same time: your Meta ad performance, competitor ads, Google search trends, live snow reports, and park event listings. Takes about 30–60 seconds.
2
All data is combined and sent to Claude AI
The collected data — your ad numbers, competitor activity, search spikes, snow conditions, and upcoming events — is packaged up and sent to Claude (an AI made by Anthropic). Claude reads all of it and writes a structured analysis: what's working, what's not, where the opportunities are, what to do.
3
The report is formatted and emailed to you
The AI's analysis is turned into the weekly HTML email report — the one in the companion file. It lands in your Gmail inbox by Sunday morning. No login required, no app to open.
4
You make the calls — system resets and waits for next Sunday
You read the report, decide which recommendations to act on, make the changes yourself in Meta Ads Manager. The system sits idle until next Sunday and repeats automatically.
Where the Data Comes From — 5 Sources
📊
Meta Ads (Your Account)
Pulls your live ad performance: spend, revenue, click rates, how often the same people see each ad, which placements and locations are working.
Uses your existing Meta account
🔎
Meta Ad Library (Competitors)
Meta's public database of all running ads. The system scans it for competitors in your category — finds what they're running, where, and how long their creatives have been live.
Free public API
📈
Google Trends
Checks search volume week-over-week for your key terms ("baggy ski pants", "snowpants", etc.) and for 36 target cities across both hemispheres. Flags anything spiking +25% or more.
Free — no API key needed
🌨️
Open-Meteo (Snow Reports)
Live snowfall data — inches in the last 7 days and total base depth — for 12 key resorts across USA, Canada, Europe, South America, NZ, and Australia. Used to prioritize which markets to spend in.
Free — no API key needed
🏂
Park Events Calendar
Scrapes the FIS Freestyle calendar for upcoming slopestyle, halfpipe, and rail jam events. Flags events within 3 weeks, calculates the ideal advertising window, and includes expected attendance.
Free public data
🧠
Claude AI (Anthropic)
All 5 data sources are combined and sent to Claude — an AI made by Anthropic. Claude reads everything and writes the analysis, recommendations, and Game Plan in plain English.
Small API cost per report (~$0.10–0.30)
What's in the Weekly Report
★Game Plan — The 5 things to do this week
Sits at the top of the report. Written for someone with no marketing background. Numbered action items with plain-English reasoning and dollar context. Takes 3 minutes to read.
1Your Ads This Week
Which ads are making money, which are losing it, which locations are converting, who's buying and when. Calls out specific ad IDs with a clear "do this" action for each.
2Market Conditions
Which hemisphere is in season, where searches are spiking vs dropping, snow conditions at 12 resorts, and all upcoming park events with their exact advertising windows.
3Competitor Activity
Where competitors are advertising (and where they're not — your gap opportunities), which of their creatives have been running long enough to be profitable, and which markets are crowded.
4AWhat to Do With Your Existing Ads
Specific instructions for each running ad: pause, scale up, clone to a new market, shift budget, adjust timing, or refine the audience. Nothing vague.
4BNew Ads Worth Launching
New markets with zero competitors, event-specific ad opportunities, new audience segments, and missing ad formats — ranked by potential profit impact.
5Big Picture
A full-market overview: everything trending up, everything trending down, hemisphere summary, event calendar, and a 2–4 week focus statement.

Common Questions
Can this system spend my money or change my ads?
No. It is completely read-only. It connects to your Meta account with a read-only access token that cannot make any changes. It only reads your data to analyze it. Any changes to your ads are made by you manually in Meta Ads Manager after reading the report.
What happens if one of the data sources is down?
The system is designed to keep running even if one source fails. If, say, Google Trends is unavailable, the rest of the report still generates normally. The report will note which source had an issue.
Can I change which markets or resorts it tracks?
Yes. The list of target cities, resorts, and competitors to watch is all configurable in the code. It can be updated anytime to reflect new markets you're entering or leaving.
Can I get the report more than once a week?
Yes. The schedule is configurable. You could run it daily if you wanted, though the data sources (especially Meta performance) are most meaningful on a weekly cadence.
Can it track more than one brand or ad account?
Not in the current setup, but it's a straightforward addition — run multiple instances with different config files.
What does the AI actually do — is it just summarizing?
It's doing more than summarizing. It receives all 5 data sources combined, plus context about the brand's three audience pillars (park riders, functional ski, streetwear/lifestyle) and hemisphere season logic. It then cross-references all of it — for example, noticing that a market has a search spike AND a competitor gap AND an upcoming event AND good snow conditions, and flagging that combination as a high-priority opportunity. A human analyst doing this manually would take 2–3 hours per week.
What if the recommendations are wrong?
The AI makes judgment calls based on the data, but you always make the final call. The report is designed to give you the reasoning behind each recommendation, not just the recommendation — so you can disagree and skip it. Over time, you can also give feedback on which recommendations worked and which didn't, and the prompts can be tuned accordingly.
